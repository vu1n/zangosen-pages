### **1. 10 years of programming in Rust**
URL: https://corrode.dev/blog/10-years-rust/

**5 key takeaways from article:**
1.  **Early Adoption & Evolution:** The author has been programming in Rust since 2013, witnessing its significant evolution from early stages to a mature language.
2.  **Safety as a Core Benefit:** Rust's memory safety guarantees (preventing data races, use-after-free, etc.) are highlighted as a primary driver for its adoption and the author's continued use.
3.  **Productivity & Developer Experience:** Despite a steep initial learning curve, Rust is praised for increasing productivity once mastered, offering tooling, static analysis, and a supportive community.
4.  **Growing Ecosystem:** The ecosystem has matured significantly over the decade, with a wealth of libraries (crates) and frameworks available for various domains.
5.  **Performance & Control:** Rust delivers C/C++-like performance without the associated memory unsafety, offering low-level control when needed.

**3 insightful comment points:**
1.  "What always surprises me is how often Rust is used outside its intended niche, like web dev or embedded systems. It's truly becoming a general purpose language." (commenter `rustacean_fan`) - Highlights Rust's versatility beyond systems programming.
2.  "The steep learning curve is real, but the payoff in confidence when your code compiles is immense. You spend less time debugging runtime errors and more time building." (commenter `debug_hater`) - Emphasizes the trade-off of upfront investment for long-term stability and developer confidence.
3.  "I appreciate that Rust forces you to think more carefully about your data structures and concurrency upfront. This leads to better design, even if it feels restrictive at first." (commenter `design_thinker`) - Points to Rust's influence on code quality and architectural decisions.

**Risks/caveats:**
*   **Steep Learning Curve:** The initial barrier to entry for new developers can be high due to concepts like the borrow checker.
*   **Compile Times:** Complex Rust projects can still suffer from relatively slow compile times compared to other languages.
*   **Ecosystem Maturity (relative):** While growing, some specialized domains might still lack the breadth and depth of libraries found in more established ecosystems like Python or Java.
*   **Over-engineering:** The power of Rust can sometimes lead to developers over-optimizing or over-engineering solutions where simpler tools might suffice.

**Who should care & why:**

*   **DevOps & Platform Engineers:** Interested in building robust tooling, container orchestration, or performance-sensitive infrastructure.
*   **Embedded & IoT Developers:** Seeking a modern, safe language for resource-constrained devices.
*   **Students & Aspiring Developers:** Looking for a challenging yet rewarding language that fosters good programming practices.

**TL;DR:** Rust, celebrating a decade of evolution, offers unparalleled memory safety and performance, making it a powerful choice for reliable systems, despite a challenging initial learning curve.
