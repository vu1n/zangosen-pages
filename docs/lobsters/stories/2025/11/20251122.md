## 1. We should all be using dependency cooldowns
**URL:** [Link](https://blog.yossarian.net/2025/11/21/We-should-all-be-using-dependency-cooldowns)

### Key Takeaways:
- **Dependency Cooldowns:** A strategy to set a time delay (7-14 days) before using new package dependencies can significantly reduce supply chain attack risks.
- **Empirical Evidence:** Most supply chain attacks occur within a week, suggesting that cooldowns can prevent the majority of these attacks.
- **Ease of Implementation:** Cooldown measures can be integrated with tools like Dependabot or Renovate easily and at minimal cost.
- **Positive Vendor Behavior:** Encouraging cooldowns shifts the focus from sensationalizing vulnerabilities to genuine detection and reporting.
- **Not a Cure-All:** Although effective against many threats, cooldowns should be part of a broader security strategy, as some attackers may find ways around them.

3. "Introduction of cooldown periods could prompt more genuine, rapid remediation by security vendors." - **criugp**

### Risks/Caveats:
- Some advanced attackers may exploit delays. Cooldowns might not prevent all vulnerabilities, especially if the attacker finds a way to bypass detection during the cooldown window.

### Who Should Care & Why:
- **Developers and Security Teams:** To adopt better practices in dependency management and increase resistance to supply chain attacks.

### TL;DR:
Adopting dependency cooldowns can effectively mitigate risks from supply chain attacks by delaying the use of new dependencies.
